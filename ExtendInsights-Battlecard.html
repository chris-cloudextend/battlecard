Objection Handling by Competitor
If the prospect says…Respond with…
"We're already using Coefficient — it connects to NetSuite."Coefficient connects to NetSuite for reading data, but their NetSuite integration is read-only — you cannot push anything back to NetSuite. Write-back in Coefficient only works for Salesforce and HubSpot. ExtendInsights gives you bulk write-back to NetSuite of up to 10,000 lines per transaction, plus AI-powered saved search queries and support from Celigo's dedicated NetSuite team. Ask: "Do you need to update records in NetSuite from your model, or just pull data out?" That question usually closes the conversation.
"Solution 7 does everything we need for NetSuite in Excel."Solution 7 is excellent for financial reporting, and if your team is purely finance, it's a solid tool. But if you also want sales, CRM, or billing data in the same report — Salesforce deals alongside your NetSuite GL — that requires ExtendInsights. Ask: "Is your NetSuite reporting only consumed by the finance team, or do ops and sales teams need visibility too?"
"We looked at Equals — it's more modern than Excel."Equals is built for SaaS startups reporting on metrics like MRR and churn. It replaces Excel entirely, which means rebuilding every template, formula, and macro your team depends on. If you're on NetSuite, Equals doesn't connect to ERP data at all. The question is whether you want to own your analysis in the tools your team already knows, or migrate and rebuild from scratch.
"Supermetrics pulls our marketing data into Excel — can't we just use that?"Supermetrics is excellent for Google Ads, Meta, and GA4 data. But it doesn't touch NetSuite, Salesforce, or your billing systems. You'd end up with marketing data in one Excel model and ERP/CRM data in another — with no way to combine them. ExtendInsights lets you build a single report that brings all of those together. Also worth noting: Supermetrics' per-source pricing tends to escalate quickly.
"Coefficient is cheaper."Coefficient's headline pricing ($49–$99/user/mo) looks lower, but NetSuite is a premium add-on with pricing that isn't published — it's not included in any standard tier. Once you add that, plus the fact that their NetSuite connector is read-only so you'll need another solution for write-back, the true cost comparison shifts significantly. Ask: "Have you gotten a quote from Coefficient that specifically includes the NetSuite connector?" That question usually reveals the gap between list price and what they'll actually pay.
"We already have NetSuite saved searches — why do we need this?"Saved searches are powerful inside NetSuite, but they don't travel well — you can't cross them with Salesforce opportunity data, apply Excel formulas and pivot tables, schedule them to run overnight, or push updated records back. ExtendInsights wraps your existing saved searches in all of those capabilities without requiring you to learn a new tool.
"We found a free tool that pulls NetSuite into Excel with no add-in — just paste a URL into Power Query."That tool uses a webhook token URL as a permanent, shareable credential for your live NetSuite data. "Cryptographically signed" sounds reassuring, but it means the URL can't be forged — it doesn't mean it can't be forwarded, appear in browser history, end up in a shared spreadsheet, or leak in a git repo. Anyone with that URL has live access to your NetSuite data with no further authentication. Ask your IT or security team how they classify live business data accessible via a URL that can be emailed to anyone. Beyond the security issue: the tool has no company behind it, no SOC 2, no support contact, no scheduling (listed as "coming soon" even on the paid tier), and is limited to 100 rows on free. It also makes several misleading comparisons about other tools on their website.
Landmines to Set & Defuse

💣 Landmines to SET (Questions That Expose Competitor Weaknesses)

  • 🎯
    For Coefficient prospects: "Do you need to push data back into NetSuite from Excel — like updated forecasts, inventory counts, or journal entries?" — Coefficient's NetSuite connector is read-only; this question immediately surfaces our unique write-back advantage.
  • 🎯
    For Solution 7 prospects: "Does your reporting need to stay within the finance team, or do ops and revenue teams need the same data?" — surfaces multi-source need.
  • 🎯
    For Equals prospects: "How many custom Excel formulas, macros, or templates does your team rely on today?" — surfaces migration cost of abandoning Excel.
  • 🎯
    For Supermetrics prospects: "When you build your pipeline or revenue report, do you need NetSuite or Salesforce data alongside your marketing spend?" — surfaces the data silo problem.
  • 🎯
    Universal: "How often does your team manually export from NetSuite and reformat in Excel?" — surfaces the time-waste that justifies the investment.
  • 🎯
    For NS Excel Connector prospects: "Does your security policy allow live business data to be accessible via a URL that can be forwarded via email or appear in browser history? Have you asked your IT or security team to review the token URL access model?" — immediately surfaces the security risk most enterprise IT teams would flag as unacceptable.

🛡️ Landmines to DEFUSE (Questions Competitors Set Against Us)

  • 🛡️
    "Does it work with Google Sheets?" Coefficient may plant this. Answer: ExtendInsights is Excel-first by design — your team gets deeper Excel functionality and Power BI integration. Google Sheets users have different tools available to them.
  • 🛡️
    "Can it connect to 100+ sources?" Coefficient may plant this. Answer: ExtendInsights covers the 6+ sources that matter most for NetSuite-centric businesses. Breadth without depth means shallow integrations.
  • 🛡️
    "Is there a free tier?" Answer: ExtendInsights offers a trial. The question is what "free" costs you in setup time, data limits, and missing NetSuite-specific features.
  • 🛡️
    "Does it have real financial drill-back to NetSuite?" Solution 7 may plant this. Be honest: Solution 7's native one-click drill-back is a genuine strength. Redirect: "Solution 7 wins on drill-back depth for pure finance reporting. Where we win is everything else — combining that NetSuite data with Salesforce, Stripe, and HubSpot in the same model, plus writing bulk updates back to NetSuite."
  • 🛡️
    "Isn't Excel outdated for analytics?" Equals may plant this. Answer: Excel remains the most widely used analytics tool globally. The question is whether your team needs to rebuild years of institutional knowledge in a new tool.
  • 🛡️
    "The NS Excel Connector says CloudExtend requires an extra NetSuite license / doesn't work on Mac / requires IT setup…" These are misleading claims. CloudExtend does not require an extra NetSuite license. CloudExtend is fully functional on Mac. On IT setup: their own Step 1 requires installing a SuiteApp — the exact same requirement they criticize CloudExtend for. Offer to verify any of these claims in writing.
